FAQ
What is a hackathon?
A hackathon is an event where students work together to create a project from scratch in a 24-hour period. Get ready to learn a lot while having fun!
What if I don't have a team or idea?
Not a problem. There will be time at the beginning of the hackathon to find other students who don't have a team yet!
Do I need to know how to code?
GenHacks is open to beginners to experienced programmers! Hackathons are a great place to learn and explore new ideas. Our mentors are always available to help, so anyone can submit a working project by the end of the hackathon, even if you’re just getting started!
Do I have to stay overnight?
Although we highly recommend it, it’s not required! You can leave at night and come back in the morning. Doors will close at 11:00 PM and open at 7:00 AM. During this time, no one will be permitted to exit or enter the building.
Still have questions?
Feel free to send us an email at genhacks2019@gmail.com or contact us via our Facebook page.
Who can come?
All high school students are welcome to attend GenHacks. If you are a college student, you can apply to be a mentor (email us at genhacks2019@gmail.com).
How much does it cost?
The event itself is completely FREE! There will be workshops, mentors, food, swag, and more for all attendees
How big can teams be?
Teams can be up to four people. While you can attend as an individual, we strongly suggest that you collaborate with at least one other person.
What should I bring?
Attendees are required to bring a valid student ID and signed waivers. A laptop, charger, sleeping bags, and toiletries are highly recommended as well.
